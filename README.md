Sales Performance Analysis — Margin Risk & Category Prioritization
Business Problem
Management had no consistent, reliable report to track monthly sales performance, margin trends, or category profitability. Decisions were being made without structured data — the goal was to identify whether performance issues were driven by demand decline or margin erosion, and prioritize categories for action.

Dataset

Tools Used

Microsoft Excel — Pivot Tables, VLOOKUP, HLOOKUP, XLOOKUP, Dynamic Charts, KPI Cards, Slicers


Approach
Built an automated Excel MIS dashboard covering three analytical dimensions:
1. Demand Analysis
Tracked units sold by month across all four quarters to determine if volume was the driver of performance changes.
2. Risk Analysis
Tracked margin % by month to identify periods of profitability pressure and isolate root cause.
3. Prioritization
Plotted Revenue vs. Margin by category to identify which categories needed immediate management attention.

Key Findings

Conclusion
Demand remained stable throughout 2024. The core business risk was margin volatility — specifically in months where margin dipped to 24.0-24.5%. Electronics was identified as the highest priority category due to its high revenue contribution combined with below-average margin, indicating a pricing or cost structure issue rather than a demand problem.

Recommendation

Investigate Electronics pricing strategy — high revenue with low margin signals either discounting pressure or cost inefficiency
Set margin floor alert at 24.5% for monthly MIS reporting — any dip below triggers review
Separate demand and margin tracking in monthly management reports — treating them together masks the real risk
